⛰️ Step 8: Sa’i – Safa & Marwa

🔹 Overview

Sa’i consists of seven rounds between Safa and Marwa, starting at Safa and ending at Marwa. It commemorates the struggle of Hajar (peace be upon her) and reflects patience, trust in Allah, and devotion.

📌 Before Starting

  • Sa’i is performed after completing Tawaf.
  • Make your intention (niyyah) sincerely for Allah.
  • Proceed calmly towards Safa.

🚪 Exiting Al-Haram Towards Sa’i

  • Leave with humility and remembrance of Allah.
  • Recommended general dhikr:
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ، تَوَكَّلْتُ عَلَى اللَّهِ، لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ

Meaning: In the name of Allah, I place my trust in Allah; there is no power nor strength except with Allah.

📍 Qur’anic Verse Before Sa’i

إِنَّ الصَّفَا وَالْمَرْوَةَ مِن شَعَائِرِ اللَّهِ ۖ فَمَنْ حَجَّ الْبَيْتَ أَوِ اعْتَمَرَ فَلَا جُنَاحَ عَلَيْهِ أَنْ يَطَّوَّفَ بِهِمَا ۚ وَمَنْ تَطَوَّعَ خَيْرًا فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ شَاكِرٌ عَلِيمٌ

Meaning: Indeed, Safa and Marwa are among the symbols of Allah. (Qur’an 2:158)

⛰️ Upon Reaching Mount Safa

  • Face the Ka'bah, raise your hands, and make dhikr and dua.
  • Recite the following:
لا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، لَهُ الْمُلْكُ وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ، وَهُوَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرُ، لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ أَنْجَزَ وَعْدَهُ، وَنَصَرَ عَبْدَهُ وَهَزَمَ الأَحْزَابَ وَحْدَهُ

Meaning: Allah is the Greatest, Allah is the Greatest, Allah is the Greatest. There is no god but Allah alone, with no partner.

  • Repeat dhikr three times and make personal supplications in between.

🚶 Walking Between Safa and Marwa

  • Walk from Safa to Marwa (1 lap).
  • Men should jog between the green markers.
  • Women walk at a normal pace.
  • Continue making dhikr and personal dua.

📍 At Marwa

  • Face the Ka'bah if possible.
  • Repeat the same dhikr and supplication as at Safa.
  • This completes one round.

🔁 Completing Seven Laps

  • Continue between Safa and Marwa until seven laps are completed.
  • The final lap ends at Marwa.

✂️ After Sa’i

  • Men shave or trim their hair.
  • Women trim a small portion of their hair.
  • This completes the Sa’i ritual.

💡 Key Notes

  • No fixed du’as are required — make personal supplications freely.
  • Wudu is recommended but not mandatory.
  • Maintain sincerity, patience, and humility throughout.
