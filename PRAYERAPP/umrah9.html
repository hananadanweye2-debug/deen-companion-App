🕋 Step 9: Concluding Umrah / Hajj

Concluding Hajj or Umrah involves specific final rituals that mark the completion of the pilgrimage, the release from the restrictions of Ihram, and a final farewell to the Kaaba.

✔️ Concluding Umrah (Lesser Pilgrimage)

  • Finalizing Sa’i: After completing seven laps between Safa and Marwa, the final lap ends at Marwa.
  • Cutting/Shaving Hair (Halq or Taqsir): Men shave their heads (preferred) or trim their hair, while women trim a small portion (1–2 cm).
  • Exiting Ihram: After cutting the hair, you may change back into normal clothing and all Ihram restrictions are lifted.
  • Optional Farewell Tawaf (Tawaf al-Wada): Recommended (sunnah) for Umrah but not mandatory.

🕋 Concluding Hajj (Major Pilgrimage)

  • Final Stoning (Rami): Stone the three Jamarat in Mina (small → middle → large).
  • Leaving Mina (Nafrah): Leave Mina for Makkah before sunset on the 12th, or stay until the 13th for extra reward.
  • Farewell Tawaf (Tawaf al-Wada): Perform seven circuits around the Kaaba — obligatory for Hajj.
  • Exemption: Women on their menstrual cycle are exempt from Tawaf al-Wada.
  • Final Supplications: Drink Zamzam, pray, and make sincere dua before departure.

💭 Final Dua

اللّهُمَّ اجعل حجي مقبولاً، وذنوبي مغفورة، وارزقني رضاك والجنة

Transliteration:

Allahumma aj‘al hajjī maqboolan, wa dhunoobī maghfoorah, warzuqnī riḍāk wal-jannah

Meaning: O Allah, accept my Hajj / Umrah, forgive my sins, and grant me Your pleasure and Paradise.

💡 General Final Actions

  • Visiting Madinah: Many pilgrims visit Masjid al-Nabawi before or after Hajj/Umrah.
  • Departure: Leave Makkah with the intention of returning and keep the Kaaba as your last sight.
  • Completion: After the Farewell Tawaf (for Hajj), the pilgrimage is fully complete.
