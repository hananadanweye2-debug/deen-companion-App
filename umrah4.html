🕋 Step 4: Entering Al-Haram

Check Your Intention

Before entering Al-Haram, take a quiet moment to reflect and renew your intention (niyyah). Ensure that your purpose for performing Umrah is purely for the sake of Allah, seeking His pleasure, mercy, and forgiveness. Let go of worldly distractions, pride, or the desire to impress others, and focus your heart on worship with sincerity and humility. Remind yourself that this is a sacred journey, a chance to strengthen your faith, purify your soul, and draw closer to Allah. Approach every step with mindfulness, gratitude, and devotion.

Practical Steps Before Entering

  • Perform Wudu (ablution) to purify yourself both physically and spiritually.
  • Approach the gates of Al-Haram with humility, focus, and sincerity, remembering you are entering a sacred place.
  • As you enter, recite the following dua:
    بِسْمِ اللهِ ، وَالصَّلَاةُ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ، اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي ذُنُوبِي، وَافْتَحْ لِي أَبْوَابَ رَحْمَتِكَ، أَعُوذُ بِاللَّهِ الْعَظِيمِ، وَبِوَجْهِهِ الْكَرِيمِ، وَبِسُلْطَانِهِ الْقَدِيمِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ
    Transliteration:
    Bismillahi, wassalatu wassalamu ‘ala Rasoolillah, Allahummaghfir li dhunoobi, waftah li abwaba rahmatik, a’udhu billahil ‘azim, wa bi wajhihil kareem, wa bi sultanihil qadeem minash shaytanir rajeem

Upon Seeing the Ka'bah

When your eyes first fall upon the Ka'bah, pause and take in this moment with a humble and heart. Raise your hands in supplication and make sincere dua, as this is a special time when prayers are deeply cherished.

You may recite Surah Al-Fatiha, then ask Allah for anything your heart desires in any language with full sincerity, hope, and trust. Let your dua come from the depths of your heart, seeking forgiveness, guidance, and blessings.

Reflect on the greatness of this moment, strengthen your connection with Allah, and begin your Umrah with humility, devotion, and gratitude.
